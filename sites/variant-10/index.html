Ten Years on the Water

A family's forty-year hospitality story, and the waterfront restaurant it built at Quarry Lake.

Chapter Two · The Story

Long before there was a Citron, there was a young Charles Levine learning the hospitality trade at Baltimore's Pimlico Hotel, absorbing the discipline and warmth that would define everything he built afterward.

In 1985, that education became Citron Caterers, an off-premise catering operation that would go on to serve nearly 14,000 events across Maryland, Washington DC, and Northern Virginia, for clients that included five United States presidents, the NFL, the Rolling Stones, and Oprah Winfrey.

For three decades, Charles and his wife Susan carried that catering business from ballroom to backyard, learning exactly what a room needs to feel like a celebration. Then, in November 2016, they brought everything they had learned home to Quarry Lake and opened Citron: a waterfront restaurant with a seasonal scratch kitchen, a full sushi bar, and a wine list built to match the view.

Ten years later, that same family still walks the dining room most nights it's open.

Chapter Three · What Ten Years Built

Four rooms, one philosophy

The Kitchen

Executive Chef Mary Hersh leads a seasonal, scratch Modern American menu alongside a full sushi bar: center-cut steaks, day-boat seafood, and house-made pastas served Wednesday through Sunday from 4 PM.

A wine program built one vintage at a time, honored with the Wine Spectator Award of Excellence every year from 2019 through 2025, and named among FINE WINE's World's Best in 2025.

03

The Water

Heated waterfront terraces and roughly 300 seats overlooking Quarry Lake, where Happy Hour runs daily from 4 to 6:30 PM with handcrafted cocktails.

04

The Family

Charles and Susan Levine still oversee the room they built, alongside a team that carries Citron Caterers' 30-plus years of off-premise hospitality into every private event.
